# IA'm — A Transparency Label for Human–AI Co-Creation

**Rethinking the human through AI. Rethinking the human for AI.**

---

## What IA'm is

IA'm is a voluntary visual label for creative works produced with the help of generative AI. It is not a quality label, not a certification, and not a watermark. It is a **presence label**: a way for the creator to declare, openly, where the creative decision came from.

## A single criterion

Three levels, one question — *who carries the creative decision?*

- **Assisté** — *Assisted.* The human creates; AI helps with discrete tasks (correction, variation, technical support). The decision remains entirely human.
- **Initié** — *Initiated.* The human directs and shapes; AI executes within a frame the human has set. The decision is shared, but human-led.
- **Généré** — *Generated.* AI produces the work from a human prompt or instruction. The decision is delegated.

A work created without AI requires no badge. The absence of IA'm is itself a statement.

## Why a creator-led label

Most current frameworks regulate the **downstream** — platforms, deployers, distribution. The EU AI Act (Article 50, applicable 2 August 2026), California SB 942, China's CAC measures, India's draft IT rules — all impose machine-readable marking and visible disclosure at the point of distribution.

These frameworks answer the question *"was this made by AI?"* They do not answer *"who decided what this work would be?"*

IA'm is a complementary layer, **upstream**: declared by the creator, before the work circulates.

## Relationship to C2PA and Content Credentials

C2PA records *what tool signed the file* and *what it was derived from*. IA'm records *who carried the creative decision*.

The two layers are compatible. A file can carry C2PA cryptographic provenance and an IA'm semantic declaration simultaneously. Where C2PA is technical and machine-readable, IA'm is semantic and human-readable.

## Founding context

IA'm originated in March 2026 as the work of a single independent creator in Brittany, France. The French trademark (INPI n° 5212301, class 41) was registered without opposition. The framework is released under Creative Commons BY-NC 4.0 — free to use, share, and adapt for non-commercial purposes, with attribution.

The project is co-created daily with AI tools and is self-declared **IA'm Initié**. The logo itself — the "m" as the irreducible human marker, contained within the letters "IA" — is used to express what it signifies, not what produced it.
